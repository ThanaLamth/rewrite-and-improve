# TheCCPress Durable Hubs and Evergreen Shortlist

Date: 2026-06-11
Domain: `https://theccpress.com/`
Purpose: choose a smaller set of durable indexable hubs and evergreen pages to support non-brand search growth

## Core Decision

Do not try to make every archive a search landing page.

The site should concentrate signals into a smaller set of durable hubs, then support those hubs with evergreen pages and stronger internal linking.

## Durable Hubs To Keep and Strengthen

These are the best candidates to remain visible, indexable search hubs based on:

- current URL stability
- current impression presence in GSC
- topic clarity
- ability to support future evergreen content

### Tier 1 Hubs

These should get the strongest internal support and long-term ownership.

1. `https://theccpress.com/latest-news/`
2. `https://theccpress.com/altcoin-news/`
3. `https://theccpress.com/crypto-101/`
4. `https://theccpress.com/learn-crypto/blockchain-technology/`

Why:

- these already appear in GSC impressions
- these are clearer than many utility or legacy buckets
- they can anchor both news and evergreen clusters

### Tier 2 Hubs

These can stay indexable, but should not receive the same level of sitewide emphasis.

1. `https://theccpress.com/press-releases/`
2. `https://theccpress.com/top-picks/crypto-exchanges/`
3. `https://theccpress.com/latest-news/bitcoin-news/`

Why:

- they match identifiable search intent
- they can absorb specific supporting content
- but they are narrower and should not dominate global navigation

## Hubs To Deprioritize

These should not be treated as major SEO landing pages right now.

1. `https://theccpress.com/news/`
2. `https://theccpress.com/cmc/`
3. coin-level or shallow archive sprawl such as `/altcoin-news/ethereum/` unless backed by real content depth
4. historical `/category/...` variants, which should fade after redirect consolidation

Why:

- weak topic differentiation
- low evidence of click generation
- risk of spreading authority too thin

## Evergreen Pages To Build or Strengthen First

These are the best next pages for non-brand demand.

### Cluster 1: Beginner Crypto Foundations

These support `crypto-101` and `learn-crypto`.

1. `what-is-cryptocurrency/`
2. `what-is-blockchain/`
3. `what-is-a-crypto-wallet/`
4. `what-is-a-crypto-exchange/`
5. `how-to-buy-crypto-safely/`

Why:

- broad non-brand query intent
- high chance of long-tail visibility
- useful for recapturing educational authority already hinted by old broken URLs

### Cluster 2: Wallets, Exchanges, and Practical Use

These support `top-picks/crypto-exchanges`, `crypto-101`, and `latest-news`.

6. `best-crypto-exchanges/`
7. `best-crypto-wallets/`
8. `custodial-vs-self-custody-wallets/`

Why:

- direct commercial-intent and comparison-intent demand
- easier to support with internal links from reviews and practical guides

### Cluster 3: Regulation and Market Structure Explainers

These support `latest-news`, future `regulation`, and higher-trust search coverage.

9. `what-is-a-crypto-press-release/`
10. `how-crypto-regulation-affects-markets/`

Why:

- helps differentiate the site from pure news churn
- creates reusable explainers for linking from breaking news articles

## Recommended URL Shape

Keep slug rules simple and stable.

Preferred model:

- short evergreen slugs
- avoid year stamps unless the page is truly time-bound
- avoid duplicate archive variants
- avoid unnecessary nested taxonomy depth unless the parent hub is clearly strategic

## Internal Linking Plan

### Strongest Sources

Use these areas to push authority into evergreen pages:

1. homepage editorial blocks
2. `latest-news/`
3. `altcoin-news/`
4. `crypto-101/`
5. `learn-crypto/blockchain-technology/`
6. relevant article bodies on regulation, wallets, exchanges, and beginner topics

### Link Targets To Prioritize First

1. `what-is-cryptocurrency/`
2. `what-is-blockchain/`
3. `what-is-a-crypto-wallet/`
4. `best-crypto-exchanges/`
5. `best-crypto-wallets/`

### Linking Rule

Do not spread sitewide links across too many weak hubs.

Instead:

- choose a few evergreen targets
- link to them repeatedly from high-crawl pages
- let those pages become secondary source pages later

## Suggested Navigation Direction

If navigation is cleaned up later, the top-level search-led structure should lean toward:

1. Latest News
2. Altcoin News
3. Crypto 101
4. Blockchain Technology
5. Press Releases

Optional secondary entry points:

1. Bitcoin News
2. Best Exchanges

## What To Avoid Next

1. Do not create many new top-level hubs before these core hubs are strengthened.
2. Do not split authority across too many coin-specific archive pages.
3. Do not rely on archive pages alone to win non-brand search.
4. Do not treat press release visibility as proof of broad topical authority.

## Short Version

If TheCCPress wants non-brand search growth, the best next move is:

1. keep a small hub set
2. support it with beginner and practical evergreen pages
3. route internal links deliberately into those pages
4. stop letting legacy and utility archives absorb crawl and signals
