# Coin Reviews Hub Page Template

Last updated: 2026-05-05

## Purpose

This template is for a `Coin Reviews` hub page that organizes token-level review content in a way that is:

- helpful to readers
- easy for Google to understand
- strong for internal linking
- scalable as Coincu publishes more coin reviews

This template is informed by official Google Search Central guidance on:

- people-first content
- site hierarchy
- crawlable internal links
- title clarity
- breadcrumb structure

Official references:

- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content

## Methodology block

This is one of the most important sections for trust and consistency.

It should explain how Coincu evaluates a coin review.

**Template**

## How Coincu Reviews a Coin

`A coin review should do more than explain why a token is trending. It should help readers understand how the token fits into a broader system and where the real strengths and weaknesses sit.`

### 1. What Is the Token?

- token name and ticker
- chain or network
- relationship between token and protocol

### 2. What Problem Is It Trying to Solve?

- product or ecosystem role
- whether the token is essential or loosely attached
- utility category such as governance, staking, access, payments, or incentives

### 3. Tokenomics and Supply Structure

- circulating supply
- max supply
- unlock schedule
- team and investor allocations
- dilution risk

### 4. Demand Drivers

- app growth
- ecosystem expansion
- staking sinks
- exchange access
- adoption catalysts

### 5. Main Risks

- execution risk
- token-demand mismatch
- liquidity risk
- concentration risk
- governance risk
- ecosystem dependency risk

# Editorial rules

- Keep the page evergreen-first.
- Use news only as selective support, not as the core of the page.
- Do not turn the hub into an automated archive.
- Keep links crawlable as normal `<a href>` links.
- Make each section genuinely useful, not decorative.
- Avoid mixing protocol, wallet, and exchange intent into the same main category blocks unless the link is clearly contextual.

---

# Best publishing checklist

- Clear title and H1
- Helpful intro written for people first
- 4 to 8 featured cornerstone reviews
- Real category structure based on reader intent
- Methodology section included
- Related hubs connected
- FAQ only if useful
- No empty sections
- No keyword stuffing
