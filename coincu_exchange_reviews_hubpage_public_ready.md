# Exchange Reviews

Choosing a crypto exchange is rarely just about finding the biggest brand. The right platform depends on what the reader actually needs: spot trading, derivatives access, beginner-friendly onboarding, deeper liquidity, lower fees, or a stronger security record.

This Exchange Reviews hub brings together Coincu's exchange coverage in one place so readers can compare trading platforms more efficiently and move from broad research into specific exchange pages with less friction. Instead of treating every exchange as interchangeable, this hub helps clarify the differences that matter most in practice.

That means looking beyond marketing claims. A useful exchange review should help readers understand fee structure, supported assets, liquidity quality, security posture, KYC requirements, regional access, and whether a platform is actually a good fit for the type of user considering it.

If you are comparing where to trade, where to park assets temporarily, or which exchange best matches your level of experience, this page is the best place to begin.

## How Coincu Reviews an Exchange

A good exchange review should help readers make a practical decision, not just repeat a platform's marketing copy.

### 1. Fees and Trading Costs

- spot fees
- derivatives fees if relevant
- spread quality where possible
- deposit and withdrawal costs

### 2. Supported Assets and Liquidity

- number and quality of supported markets
- whether the exchange lists major assets, long-tail assets, or both
- liquidity depth for the types of trades readers are likely to make

### 3. Security and Operational Trust

- custody model
- proof-of-reserves or transparency signals
- account security features
- incident history if relevant

### 4. Regulation, KYC, and Regional Access

- where the exchange operates
- whether KYC is mandatory
- access restrictions by jurisdiction
- practical availability for real users

### 5. Product Depth and User Fit

- whether the platform fits beginners, active traders, or advanced users
- spot, margin, futures, staking, earn, or launchpad tools
- whether the workflow is simple enough for the intended audience

### 6. Main Risks

- custody risk
- counterparty risk
- compliance and regional restrictions
- thin liquidity in smaller markets
- complexity risk for newer users

## FAQ

### What makes a good crypto exchange review?

A good exchange review should explain fees, asset access, security features, liquidity, regional restrictions, and who the platform is actually best suited for.

### Which exchange reviews should beginners start with?

Begin with Coincheck, P2B, and Tapbit if you want simpler starting points, then compare them against larger platforms like Binance or KuCoin once you know what features matter most.

### Are all exchange reviews meant for the same kind of user?

No. Some exchanges are better for beginners, some for altcoin breadth, and some for advanced trading. That is why the hub separates reviews by intent and use case.

### Are exchange reviews financial or legal advice?

No. Exchange reviews are informational research pages. Readers should still check local compliance, custody risk, and platform suitability for their own situation.
